A Fascination with Stories

Welcome!

I am Newt, a strange creature searching for meaning in a life defined by meaninglessness. I've been fascinated by stories of all kinds, and use them as a way to derive meaning. I started life as a programmer, but have since turned into more of a researcher and writer. While I'm fascinated by stories of all kinds and mediums, I am trying to teach myself the art of adaptation rather than creation. My interest in how these tales mutate with each retelling, and as such I do a lot of writing on established works.

Multifaceted and Flexible

I went to college for programming, but have always had a love of art. I've spent some time doing 3D modeling, drawing and even some real life sculpting. During my time as a Twitch streamer I put my abilities as a writer to use, allowing me to craft a world that my stream's events took place in. As a YouTube creator I started to use Gimp more often, and now have a decent understanding of how to make professional looking thumbnails for the purposes of attracting attention. On top of all of this, I spend plenty of time writing and reviewing scripts and working with others on videos. None of my projects are solo, though I arguably have the largest part in each one.

Problem-solver at Heart

Due to my large number of interests, I'm always driven to learn new skills. This means I'm constantly facing situations or problems I've never seen before, or looking back at issues I once thought impossible with my current skill set. I despise working on the same rote issues day after day, which has led me to quit a few jobs once I felt I wasn't learning anything new from them.

Dedication to the Craft

Regardless of what I do, I do not do so half-heartedly. I have been known to spend years on my passion projects. I don't expect that to change anytime soon. Works on projects like my Lobotomy Corporation story retrospective took over a year, and if I'm capable to doing projects like this while still feeding myself, I absolutely will.

The Dark Forest

The Dark Forest

The Dark Forest is a world created alongside my Twitch streams. While it initially began as a goofy little side project, it grew into a pretty large idea. It was my first world to be created in WorldAnvil, and it's still occasionally being added to today. It helped me theme many of the events I used for my stream, and it's where the Campgrounds dwell.

Its main storyline chronicles the life of Zephnos, a not-so-happy forest creature tasked with keeping humans away from the Campgrounds. Created to be a captive audience, he's nonetheless conscripted by the Dark Forest itself to act as a guardian for the Campgrounds -- but he's never able to come into the light himself. Over time, it's revealed that much of Zephnos' history is repeating, and it's this repetition that began my interest in story mutation. Zephnos and the forest he dwells in are both very personal to me, even if I don't think they're exactly unique. Indeed, that lack of uniqueness is somewhat the point, as the Dark Forest is known for its tendency to harvest stories and characters.

Moonsreach

A frozen, sunless planet

Moonsreach is my second WorldAnvil world. Connected to the Dark Forest through a single character, it's easily the most alien story idea I've ever had... in that all of its characters are literally aliens.

Developed over the course of a single weekend, Moonsreach actually has a complete history from start to finish. This was made much easier through collaboration with my close friend iZ, who helped me hammer out many ideas incredibly fast. While Moonsreach could absolutely be fleshed out more, it was an important step towards understanding the Dark Forest multiverse.

Moonsreach established that the gods in this setting are predatory, and see most sentient life as nothing more a way to accumulate more and more power as they exist. They continue to raise civilizations not out of pride or benevolence, but because it benefits them in the long run. It's this fact that sets the tone for the Dark Forest's gods.

This is by far my most interesting idea, and one I'd love to flesh out more if I ever found the time. As a world with three completely different alien races who depend on each other to survive, something about it just feels... compelling. Add into this that the planet has no sun and is completely tied to its orbiting lunar goddesses for survival, and there's a ton to play with.

And then of course there's the extra idea that Moonsreach is a massive water world. Completely sheathed in ice all the way up to the barely-present atmosphere, it's a world of ridiculous extremes. Without divine intervention, life would struggle to exist here at all.

The Stone of Stories

A title reused from an idea with the Dark Forest, the Stone of Stories is the name I've given to my collection of notes that I make publicly available. These notes track the ideas and thoughts I have as I research various video topics, and are available for anyone interested in how the sausage is made. I'm still working through the display process on this, so it's liable to change over time.

The Campgrounds

The Dark Forest's safe haven

, The Campgrounds houses the Salamander and its fire. Rules of the Dark Forest prevent the monsters that stalk it from entering the light, meaning it's the only place people who fall into it can spend the night without fear. That's changed over time, but this is a story that never fully met its end... fittingly, given that its themes lie in stagnation, corruption of purpose, and an acceptance of the status quo. Thanks to the events of the Dark Forest, nothing ever truly dies.

This was initially concieved as a way to explain the story events within my Twitch streams, but grew into far more than that. I've tried to tell the Salamanders' (and Zephnos') story three separate times, but something always stops it. Maybe I'll resume it again sometime in the future.
